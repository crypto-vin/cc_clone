It’s been nine years since a few Stanford professors decided to offer their courses online for free. These later became known as MOOCs, for Massive Open Online Courses . And 2012 became the ‘ Year of the MOOC ’.

Well, 2020 might be MOOCs’ next most consequential year. The COVID-19 pandemic has boosted learners’ interest in online education. MOOC providers, in particular, have seen a significant traffic influx, as previously covered by Class Central’s Report .

The pandemic has also impacted the most popular list. Compared to last year’s 100 most popular courses , the number of health and medicine online courses has gone up.

In fact, 18 courses in the ranking are directly related to COVID. The top course, with over 1 million enrollments, is Johns Hopkins’ Covid-19 संपर्क अनुरेखण . It is followed by Harvard’s कोविड -19 के लिए यांत्रिक वेंटिलेशन , with 300K enrollments.
